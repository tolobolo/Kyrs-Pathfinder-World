
## The Assembly of High Thrones (AHT)

The Assembly of High Thrones was established in the wake of the devastating global wars that followed the collapse of the Tri-Kin Hegemony. It functions as the world's primary diplomatic body, similar to a global parliament or United Nations, with the goal of maintaining absolute stability. Every kingdom on the continent is a member, as the organization’s primary objective is to remove any threat that could break the world’s balance without favoring one member over another.

The AHT operates under a comprehensive rulebook that mirrors modern international law, governing everything from trade rights in the Paso de Lath to the regulation of necromancy under the Grave-Hallow Accord. Headquartered in the Levalon Dynasty, the Assembly serves as the neutral ground where emperors and democratically elected leaders must meet to resolve disputes. While the AHT does not have a massive standing army of its own, its influence is absolute; any nation that violates the rulebook faces total economic isolation and the combined pressure of every other member-state.

In recent years, the AHT has established several official organs to oversee its interests, most notably the Sovereign Purveyors (PS). While the PS serves as a direct administrative arm of the government, the AHT has also strategically empowered the Dwarven clan Asvhein as an independent party. By granting the clans special authority and resources, the AHT has enabled them to compete directly against the Levelion Dynasty, successfully breaking their monopoly over trade routes. This move highlights how the AHT uses a combination of its own departments and external alliances to maintain a balance of power and ensure peace through economic competition.

## The Southern Alliance

While the AHT is for diplomacy, the Southern Alliance is a focused military and political pact. It was originally formed as a coalition to defend against any single kingdom attempting to conquer the continent. Currently, the alliance is a permanent partnership between the Levalon Dynasty and the Khennuirian Empire.

The Southern Alliance represents a "Peace through Strength" philosophy. It was the driving force behind the modernization of the world, using Dwarven firearms and Khennuirian industrial technology to counter the magical dominance of the older empires. In terms of military power, the Southern Alliance is ranked as the second-largest force in the world, surpassed only by the Xeolia Empire. Its primary mission is to act as a deterrent; if any nation—most notably the expansionist Xeolia—were to attempt a full-scale invasion of the central lands, the Southern Alliance is treaty-bound to respond with the full weight of the Khennuirian military and Levalon's strategic fortifications.

## The Ministry of Souls and the Grave-Hallow Accord


The final piece of the global political puzzle is the Ministry of Souls. Born from the first wars against the Trolls, this alliance of necromancers, vampires, and undead creatures was once a secret society. Because they were too powerful to be destroyed without a world-ending war, the Assembly of High Thrones signed the Grave-Hallow Accord.

This treaty legalized necromancy under strict regulations. The Ministry is permitted to exist as long as they only use "already dead" bodies, creating a regulated market where families can sell the remains of deceased loved ones for gold. In exchange, the Ministry provides the world with a tireless, undead labor force and magical research. Though they have a hidden school in the Khennuirian mountains, they are officially recognized diplomats, making them a "necessary evil" in the modern political machine.

## The Sovereign Purveyors (PS)

Originally, bounty hunting was a tool used by revolutionaries to dismantle the Tri-Kin Hegemony by picking off its leaders. However, after the empire fell, the hunters became too powerful and disorganized, threatening the stability of the new nations. To bring them to heel, the empires established The Sovereign Purveyors (PS).

What began as a government oversight agency has evolved into a global monopoly. Today, the PS is a massive organization that controls the legal right to hunt. To work as a bounty hunter, you must pay for a membership and follow their strict protocols. They take a percentage of every contract and have the power to "blackball" any hunter, effectively making them a criminal in the eyes of all four empires. Only a few individuals—those with immense reputations like Scrub "Click"—have ever been strong enough to operate outside the PS without being hunted down themselves. The PS functions almost like a fifth kingdom, with its own internal laws and a reach that extends into every dark corner of the continent.

## The Dwarven Clan Network

The Dwarven people are not a single nation but a collection of powerful clans that control the world’s most vital resources. While they are spread across different empires, they are bound by the Treaty of Mount Dhor, which makes them a unified political force that no human or elf king can ignore.

  ### Ironforge & Coalfire (The Volcanic Core):
    Based in and around Volcan Dhur, these clans are the heart of the world's industry. The Ironforge Clan is the dominant political force, effectively controlling the other clans. Together with the Coalfire Clan, they operate the main forges of the world. Because they control the elemental cooling wards, they hold the Zozzalyra Kingdom in a state of constant diplomatic debt.

  ### Asvhein (The Gatekeepers):
    Located in the Paso de Lath, the Asvhein Clan controls the "Trade Pass." They do not build or mine as much as others; instead, they tax every shipment of timber, grain, and ore that moves between the center and the northeast. They are the wealthiest clan and act as the world’s primary bankers.

  ### Tongforge & Iceforge (The Imperial Clans): 
    The Tongforge are the muscle of the clans, located in the Khennuirian Empire, providing the man-power and heavy infantry that keep the North stable. In the Xeolia Empire, the Iceforge Clan specializes in mining the frozen peaks and creating "cold weapons" that can freeze targets on impact.

  ### Stonehammer: 
    This clan is more reclusive, focusing on deep-earth masonry and the structural integrity of the mountain ranges, often serving as the primary architects for the world’s greatest fortifications.
