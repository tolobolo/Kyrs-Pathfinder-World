# Technology


## Technology to day
Aethgard is a land powered by advanced technology, but its primary resources are magical. Because most systems rely on magic to function, a deep split has formed in society. Magic users are typically seen as more valuable and occupy higher social classes than those without arcane abilities. The only commoners who manage to reach these high social circles are typically wealthy individuals or those who have achieved great personal feats.

### Vehicles 
in Aethgard are powered by either magic or gas, though gas is rarely used because magic is far more efficient and gas mining is limited to specific regions. Magic-run cars require a spellcaster to operate, making them a luxury of the upper class. In recent years, these vehicles have seen heavy military use because they are more robust than horses; newer models have even been outfitted with mounted weaponry. While both Xeolia and Levelon produce these machines, Levelon refuses to manufacture gas cars because the emissions and extraction methods actively harm the surrounding nature.

### Flying ships 
are another common form of transportation, though they are rarely owned privately. Instead, they serve as the "buses" or "taxis" of the empires, moving people and goods across the world. While useful, they are notoriously slow and fragile, making them highly vulnerable to strong winds and storms. In military circles, they are used for troop transport, and engineers have recently begun experimenting with mounting the new guns onto their hulls. These ships are manufactured globally, but Zozzalyra is the leading producer, as their civilization is almost entirely reliant on them.

### Ships 
are among the oldest technologies still in use, primarily for global trade. They remain a staple of military forces because they are far less dependent on unpredictable weather than flying ships. While traditional sails still dominate, newer, smaller vessels have begun using magic as a power source instead of the wind, marking the beginning of a new era in naval engineering.

### Carriages 
Remain in common use because, unlike complex cars that can break down, they are simple and durable. Many have been upgraded with small auxiliary motors to help the animals pull through deep snow or difficult terrain. However, because they lack the armor and speed of the newer vehicles, they are rarely used in military operations.

### The Forges
located primarily in Zozzalyra and scattered across the world, serve as the backbone of civilization. They are the primary source of all manufactured goods. To ensure these vital assets are never lost, the AHT  enforces the Treaty of Magma-Reach, a global pact that forbids attacks on Forge sites and prevents anyone from sabotaging them. Because these Forges are fueled by raw magma, they are operated almost exclusively by Kobolds and Dwarves. However, the Ratfolk have recently begun experimenting with their own unique forging techniques.

### Automason
Zelmet’s process, known as Aetheric Binding, fused reinforced cobalt-alloy frames with a specialized "Heart-Core" vessel. This core housed a stabilized elemental essence, protected by silver-etched runes to prevent volatile surges or demonic corruption. By integrating spectral-optics and a "Siphon-Lamp" directly into their chassis, he enabled these units to track abyssal heat signatures and physically vacuum demonic energy into internal containment chambers for permanent incineration.

## Older years

While the ancient kingdoms of the Dragons and Elves may not have been as industrial, they were masters of high magic. One of the most famous feats of non-industrial technology was the Dragon Flight-Cities. Built from earth and held aloft by pure magic, they were a monumental achievement; however, they were all destroyed during the first Troll-Dragon War. The dragons were never able to reconstruct them, and Aethgard has never seen their like since.

Another vital invention was the Warforged Giants. Powered by spinning magical cores, these behemoths were first introduced by the Trolls, but the Elves halted their production and burned the recipes and lore to prevent more from being built. Despite this, a few still exist deep within the world. Two of these giants are currently being experimented on by the Kennurian Empire in an attempt to rediscover the secrets of their creation.
