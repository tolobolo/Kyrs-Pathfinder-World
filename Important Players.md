# Important Players of History

## Izar the Conqueror

Born into the high nobility of the Tri-Kin Hegemony, Izar was a lizarfolk strategic genius and a staunch supporter of the regime. During the twilight of the Hegemony, he sought immortality and power, eventually joining a secret cabal of vampires and necromancers. After falling in a skirmish against Elven and Dwarven resistance fighters, Izar used dark magic to transform himself into a Lich, though the ritual forced him into a deep stasis to heal his broken form.

When he finally emerged, the world he knew was gone. He found a small, chaotic war brewing in the Northeast. Hiding his undead nature and his growing necromantic power from the public, he rose to lead the local armies to a total victory. He often won battles without losing a single living soldier, secretly deploying legions of zombies under the cover of night. Though he initially sought total conquest, he eventually married a vampire and had children through powerful blood-magic, leading him to focus on stability over war. This shift birthed the Khennuirian Empire. To prevent his kingdom from collapsing after his final death—caused by a divine death ray from a high priest—he decreed in his will that the empire become a democracy, stripping his own royal family of power.

## Zelmet Iron-Whisper
Hailing from the Zozzalyra Empire, the gnome inventor Zelmet Iron-Whisper began his career fascinated by the ancient history of bound elementals. After studying as a professional inventor, he spent years working in an airship factory before branching out to pitch his own private Warforged company. Though his homeland of Zozzalyra ultimately rejected his ambitious proposal, the Khennurian Empire stepped in with a massive funding offer, provided he relocate and build a mechanical army to eradicate the world's remaining demons.

Zelmet accepted the deal and successfully engineered the twenty legendary automaton battalions that ended the Age of Terror. Following the war, he transitioned into the automotive industry, though his car designs have yet to see a major commercial breakthrough. Today, he remains in the Khennurian Empire, where he oversees his private company and lives a quiet, successful life with his family.

## Scrub "Click"

A Ratfolk born into slavery years after the rise of the great empires, Scrub’s life changed on his third birthday when he escaped captivity by killing the scientist holding him hostage. While he escaped with his siblings, his parents were not as fortunate. While his brother entered the world of politics, Scrub entered the world of blood, joining the Sovereign Purveyors (PS) as a bounty hunter.

He earned the nickname "Click" because his targets usually heard only a single mechanical click before their lives ended. An expert alchemist, he developed specialized ammunitions that could pierce magical defenses. His most famous feat was the assassination of Emperor Surzun II of the Xeolia Empire, an act that effectively near colapst the widespread slavery of Ratfolk and Kobolds in the region. Despite rumors that he killed Izar the Conqueror, the two were actually close friends; Izar’s will even tasked Click with "one last job"—the execution of Izar’s own power-hungry son, Tsaen. Click died in a final confrontation with Emperor Surzun III, succumbing to exhaustion, but he died smiling knowing his death was being filmed to ensure his legend—and the fear he struck into tyrants—would live forever.

## Klack the Detective

Klack was a Kobold born at the dawn of the new age with a mind for puzzles and a disregard for the law. In his youth, he worked as a freelance detective, frequently "jumping rope" with legal boundaries to solve cases. His life changed after a case involving a mysterious spy; he fell in love and married her in secret, an event that led him to moderate his criminal tendencies and join the Kobold Independence Front (KIF).

Though he advocated for a peaceful Kobold state, his lack of success in mainstream politics led him to found the Kobold Detective Firm (KDT). On the surface, it was a small investigation agency, but underneath, it was the world’s most efficient spy network. Klack used the intel gathered by KDT to help allies like Scrub "Click" and to dismantle the careers of corrupt officials. He died as he lived—in the middle of a high-stakes investigation—being defending at a grand party by an assassin.

## Dralf Ironforge

The heir to the Northern Dwarven throne, Dralf grew up during the civil wars that followed the collapse of the Tri-Kin Hegemony. Disgusted by how generals treated Dwarven, Orcish, and Troll lives as expendable, he vowed to be a different kind of ruler. After ascending to the throne, he successfully conquered the Volcan Dhur region.

Rather than declaring himself Emperor of a second Great Dwarven Empire, Dralf showed incredible restraint. Recognizing that a single crown could not hold the world together, he instead created the Clans System and authored the Treaty of Mount Dhor. This treaty transformed a military alliance into a political and economic one, bringing the world closer together. His peaceful diplomacy even led the southwestern Dwarven clans to join the Levalon Dynasty willingly. Dralf eventually chose to pass away from old age, leaving a legacy of stability rather than conquest.

## Mantus the Dark

Born in the Dhor mountains, Mantus was a veteran of the first Dragon-Troll wars. Obsessed with achieving a 100% win rate in battle, he turned to necromancy after being tipped off by a Goblin gang. He famously led an undead army to victory in the Forge and Fire War against the Dwarves.

His thirst for war was unquenchable. After massacring a peaceful Dwarven settlement in the desert, he was confronted by a flight of Dragons intended to stop his rampage. Through a combination of raw power and dark diplomacy, he convinced many of the Dragons to join him and study necromancy. This sparked a Dragon Civil War and an age of undead dominance. In his final moments, as his physical form was defeated, he funneled his immense magical reserves into a ritual, ascending to become a Spirit God.

## Sylvaris: The Ethereal Engine

In his youth, Sylvaris was a prodigy of the Elven spires, obsessed with the "Unmaking." While his peers studied creation, he perfected Abjuration and Nullification, eventually discovering the legendary "Void-Thread"—a unique form of magic capable of dispelling even the most ancient enchantments. This brilliance became his curse when he was captured during a Hobgoblin raid. Recognizing his genius, the later tri-kin hermony forced him into magical servitude, shackling his mind to their war effort. He spent decades as their "Architect of the Unstoppable," forced to enchant their massive flying war machines with layers of void-shielding that made them immune to any conventional magical defense.

Desperate to escape his role as a weapon of mass destruction, Sylvaris turned his research inward. In a final, defiant act of sabotage, he initiated the Ritual of the Severed Soul. As his physical body perished, he didn't simply die; he transformed into a Spiritual God of the Weave. Now existing as a sentient mass of anti-magic, he haunts the very sky-fleets he was forced to create. Legends say that when the Hobgoblin engines fail without explanation, it is the spirit of Sylvaris reaching out from the Ethereal Plane to unmake the work of his own hands.

## Arakour Cragarmour: The Steel Sovereign
Born to a failing throne, Arakour traded dwarven tradition for cold arcana and industrial might. To prevent his father’s stagnation, he seized power, crushed internal dissent, and launched a global war of conquest. He revolutionized dwarven history by blending forbidden magic with technology, leading to the "Flying Ship Experiment"—the birth of elemental-powered dreadnoughts that broke the ancient religious laws of the forge.

Arakour’s ambition didn't end with his flesh. In his final moments, he funneled his vast knowledge into a dark ritual, shedding his mortal body to become a Spirit-God of Gear and Ghost. Now, he exists as a divine engine, haunting the very machines he built and demanding eternal labor from those who still serve the Cragarmour name.
