# Important Players of History

## Izar the Conqueror

Born into the high nobility of the Tri-Kin Hegemony, Izar was a strategic genius and a staunch supporter of the regime. During the twilight of the Hegemony, he sought immortality and power, eventually joining a secret cabal of vampires and necromancers. After falling in a skirmish against Elven and Dwarven resistance fighters, Izar used dark magic to transform himself into a Lich, though the ritual forced him into a deep stasis to heal his broken form.

When he finally emerged, the world he knew was gone. He found a small, chaotic war brewing in the Northeast. Hiding his undead nature and his growing necromantic power from the public, he rose to lead the local armies to a total victory. He often won battles without losing a single living soldier, secretly deploying legions of zombies under the cover of night. Though he initially sought total conquest, he eventually married a vampire and had children through powerful blood-magic, leading him to focus on stability over war. This shift birthed the Khennuirian Empire. To prevent his kingdom from collapsing after his final death—caused by a divine death ray from a high priest—he decreed in his will that the empire become a democracy, stripping his own royal family of power.

## Scrub "Click"

A Ratfolk born into slavery years after the rise of the great empires, Scrub’s life changed on his third birthday when he escaped captivity by killing the scientist holding him hostage. While he escaped with his siblings, his parents were not as fortunate. While his brother entered the world of politics, Scrub entered the world of blood, joining the Sovereign Purveyors (PS) as a bounty hunter.

He earned the nickname "Click" because his targets usually heard only a single mechanical click before their lives ended. An expert alchemist, he developed specialized ammunitions that could pierce magical defenses. His most famous feat was the assassination of Emperor Surzun II of the Xeolia Empire, an act that effectively ended the widespread slavery of Ratfolk and Kobolds in the region. Despite rumors that he killed Izar the Conqueror, the two were actually close friends; Izar’s will even tasked Click with "one last job"—the execution of Izar’s own power-hungry son, Tsaen. Click died in a final confrontation with Emperor Surzun III, succumbing to exhaustion, but he died smiling knowing his death was being filmed to ensure his legend—and the fear he struck into tyrants—would live forever.

## Klack the Detective

Klack was a Kobold born at the dawn of the new age with a mind for puzzles and a disregard for the law. In his youth, he worked as a freelance detective, frequently "jumping rope" with legal boundaries to solve cases. His life changed after a case involving a mysterious spy; he fell in love and married her in secret, an event that led him to moderate his criminal tendencies and join the Kobold Independence Front (KIF).

Though he advocated for a peaceful Kobold state, his lack of success in mainstream politics led him to found the Kobold Detective Firm (KDT). On the surface, it was a small investigation agency, but underneath, it was the world’s most efficient spy network. Klack used the intel gathered by KDT to help allies like Scrub "Click" and to dismantle the careers of corrupt officials. He died as he lived—in the middle of a high-stakes investigation—defending a grand party from an assassin.

Dralf Ironforge

The heir to the Northern Dwarven throne, Dralf grew up during the civil wars that followed the collapse of the Tri-Kin Hegemony. Disgusted by how generals treated Dwarven, Orcish, and Troll lives as expendable, he vowed to be a different kind of ruler. After ascending to the throne, he successfully conquered the Volcan Dhur region and secured the Paso de Lath.

Rather than declaring himself Emperor of a second Great Dwarven Empire, Dralf showed incredible restraint. Recognizing that a single crown could not hold the world together, he instead created the Clans System and authored the Treaty of Mount Dhor. This treaty transformed a military alliance into a political and economic one, bringing the world closer together. His peaceful diplomacy even led the southwestern Dwarven clans to join the Levalon Dynasty willingly. Dralf eventually chose to pass away from old age, leaving a legacy of stability rather than conquest.

Mantus the Dark

Born in the Dhor mountains, Mantus was a veteran of the first Dragon-Troll wars. Obsessed with achieving a 100% win rate in battle, he turned to necromancy after being tipped off by a Goblin gang. He famously led an undead army to victory in the Forge and Fire War against the Dwarves.

His thirst for war was unquenchable. After massacring a peaceful Dwarven settlement in the desert, he was confronted by a flight of Dragons intended to stop his rampage. Through a combination of raw power and dark diplomacy, he convinced many of the Dragons to join him and study necromancy. This sparked a Dragon Civil War and an age of undead dominance. In his final moments, as his physical form was defeated, he funneled his immense magical reserves into a ritual, ascending to become a Spirit God.

The Khennuirian Empire

Situated in the frozen, snowy mountain ranges of the Northwest, the Khennuirian Empire is a paradoxical state that functions as a democracy while maintaining a rigid, Spartan military culture. Founded by the necromancer Izar the Conqueror, the nation overlooks the northern seas where dragons still roam the skies. Politically, the empire leans toward the right, serving as a massive industrial hub where numerous global companies are headquartered. Their military philosophy is reminiscent of a more technologically advanced Normandy, as they invest heavily in new weaponry and magical engineering. Uniquely, they actively recruit soldiers from outside their borders, offering a path to citizenship through service, a practice that ensures their legions are always replenished with fresh perspectives and diverse skills.
The Xeolia Empire

In the lush, forested highlands of the Northeast, the Xeolia Empire surrounds the vast Lago de Lharat. It is governed by a dictatorial monarchy that curiously respects a democratic parliament, though the Emperor’s word remains the ultimate authority. This right-wing empire is built on the belief of racial superiority, specifically favoring humans, dwarves, and elves. The nation has a long, bloody history of dictators, transitioning from Hobgoblin rule to the current Human-Hogoblin lineage of the Kuzun-Rai dynasty. Their military is famous for its massive cavalry divisions and a brutal "no retreat" policy, viewing any withdrawal as a sign of terminal weakness. Despite their strength, they are currently plagued by persistent troll uprisings in the northern reaches of their territory.
The Levalon Dynasty

The Levalon Dynasty controls the dense forests and green heartlands of the continent's center, including the strategically vital Torre de Lath and the central mountain pass. As a neutral democracy, the Dynasty prioritizes peace and maintains the lowest military spending of the four powers, relying instead on ancient fortifications left behind by the old Goblin kingdoms. It serves as the world’s primary manufacturer for the Assembly of High Thrones (AHT) and the Southern Alliance. While the nation is a melting pot of humans and other races, a rising right-wing elven political party has begun to gain traction, advocating for the removal of non-elves and the restoration of what they believe to be the rightful elven hegemony over the central forests.
The Zozzalyra Kingdom

Located in the arid, pyramid-studded deserts of the Southeast, the Zozzalyra Kingdom sits in the dangerous shadow of Volcan Dhur. It is a left-wing democracy, though the government is frequently dogged by suspicions of election cheating and corruption. Following a catastrophic volcanic eruption, the kingdom’s original mountain settlements were abandoned to fire dwarves and elementals, forcing the populace into the desert lowlands. Their military strategy is one of attrition, often characterized by throwing one army after another at a problem until it is resolved. Because of their proximity to the volcano, the kingdom’s survival is entirely dependent on complex trade agreements and the elemental wards maintained by the dwarves who still inhabit the volcanic peaks.





The global political balance is maintained by two distinct yet interconnected organizations: one designed for world-wide diplomacy and another for collective military defense. While they often work together to prevent a return to the "Age of the Sky-Empire," their goals and memberships differ significantly.

The Global Military Hierarchy

To understand the weight of these organizations, the world's military power is currently ranked as follows:

    Xeolia Empire (Massive cavalry and "no retreat" tradition)

    The Southern Alliance (The combined might of Khennuirian tech and Levalon defense)

    Khennuirian Empire (Individually powerful, focuses on high-tech and legionnaires)

    Levalon Dynasty (Focuses on ancient defensive structures and AHT peacekeeping)

    The Assembly of High Thrones (A small, specialized peacekeeping force)
