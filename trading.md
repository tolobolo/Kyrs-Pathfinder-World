# Trading

Trading in Kyros normally happens via flyship, but because of their dependence on favorable weather, traditional ships are still used for their flexibility. On land, trade generally moves by train, guarded by bounty hunters under PS (Sovereign Purveyors). These bounty hunters are cost-effective and highly efficient against the bandits who challenge this mode of transportation. For short-distance travel, horse-drawn carriages remain the standard, though they are slowly being replaced by cars.

## The Paso de Lath Conflict

The Paso de Lath is the primary route used by northern traders to reach the Zozzalyra Kingdom, but this passage is strictly controlled by Xeolia. Relations between the northern merchants and Xeolia are currently poor, characterized by deep-seated animosity and a lack of diplomatic "good faith." This tension turns the pass into a major geopolitical bottleneck, where traders are forced to deal with a hostile power that holds all the leverage over their most important route.

## Hazards of Kennuirian Trade

Trading through the Kennuirian regions is a high-risk endeavor defined by constant physical danger. Merchants attempting this path are frequently targeted by merfolk, sea serpents, and pirates, making the waters some of the most treacherous in the world. Despite these biological and criminal threats, a shift is coming: new technology is currently being developed to counter these hazards, promising a future where the sea is no longer an impassable barrier for commerce.

## The Levelon Monopoly

Due to its position at the center of the map, Levelon maintains a dominant grip on the majority of global trade. This central monopoly has become a point of contention for the Assembly of High Thrones (AHT), which is now taking active steps to dismantle Levelon's economic shadow. As part of this strategic move, the AHT is utilizing the Asvhein dwarven clan to create new avenues of commerce, specifically designed to bypass Levelon and redistribute economic power.
