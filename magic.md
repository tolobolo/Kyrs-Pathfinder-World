### Magic
In the world of the fourth planet, magic is not a singular force but a layered progression of energy that has evolved alongside civilization. It is categorized into four primary disciplines: Primary, Arcana, Divine, and Occult. Each represents a different era of understanding and a different relationship with the fundamental laws of reality.
The Primordial Root: Primary Magic

## Primary magic:
is the raw, unrefined precursor to all other disciplines. It is the magic of the world's first breath—the untamed energy of the three moons, the shifting tectonic plates, and the violent atmospheric winds. This is the magic of the Trolls and the Dragons, existing in the world long before Elves or Dwarves arrived to categorize it. This power is not learned through books; it is felt in the blood and the bones. It is instinctive and inherently dangerous, manifesting today through the Orcish Druids who command the wilds and the Fire Dwarves who use Primal resonance to speak directly to the molten heart of Volcan Dhur.
The Language of the World: Arcana

## Arcana:
is the foundational form of magic used by modern civilization. Often referred to as the "Language of the World," it is the raw manipulation of the elements and the ley lines that crisscross the planet. To an Arcanist, magic is a science, and the world is a machine waiting to be programmed with complex formulas and precise gestures. Necromancy exists as a specialized sub-class within Arcana; because life and death are considered natural cycles of the world’s energy, a master of Arcana can learn to tap into the "echoes" of that energy to animate bone and flesh.
The Ascended Will: Divine Magic

## Divine magic:
is the second youngest discipline, and its origin is uniquely tied to the history of the sentient races. Unlike other worlds where gods are eternal creators, the gods of this world were once living beings—powerful mages like Arakour Cragarmour or Izar the Conqueror—who used immense rituals to shed their physical forms and become spirits of pure energy. Divine magic is the process of drawing power from these "Spirit Gods." It is a magic of faith and connection, primarily used for healing, protection, and the "Hallowing" process that keeps the undead labor force docile and safe for the public.

## Occult magic:
is the youngest and most unpredictable branch of the mystic arts. It is essentially "Modern Arcana"—the result of experimentalists and rebels who grew tired of the rigid laws of ancient Elven schools. Occultism is born from trial and error, often involving the "stretching" of Arcane formulas until they produce strange, unintended effects. It is favored by the Gnomes and the Khennuirian Empire, who seek to merge magic with new technology. Because it involves experimenting with the unknown, Occultism is prone to "magical bleeding," where the user’s own mind or body can be warped by the unstable energies they summon.

## Necromancy:
While often feared, Necromancy is the bridge that connects all three disciplines. An Arcanist views Necromancy as the manipulation of biological energy; a Divine caster views it as the transition of the soul; and an Occultist views it as a way to "hack" the cycle of life to create a tireless workforce. This universal presence is why the Grave-Hallow Accord is so vital to global politics—because magic is everywhere, the potential for the dead to rise is a constant reality that every nation must legally and magically prepare for.
